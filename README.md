## 📖 Overview

Phishing attacks are one of the most significant cybersecurity threats facing organizations today. These attempts use deceptive emails, messages, or calls to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords, financial data, or personal details, or to install malware.

This Phishing Awareness System is designed to educate employees on how to identify, analyze, safely handle, and report potential phishing attacks. By increasing our collective vigilance, we can significantly reduce the risk of falling victim.

**🎯 Key Goals:**
* Empower every individual to recognize phishing attempts.
* Clearly understand the common indicators and psychological tactics used.
* Provide clear, standard procedures for safe handling and reporting.
* Reduce successful phishing attempts and the associated risks.

### 1. 🎁 Lottery / Prize Scam Email (Phishing)

**Scenario:** You receive an unexpected email claiming you've won a large sum of money in a lottery, prize draw, or sweepstakes.

**🚩 Key Phishing Indicators:**
* **Fake Sender Domain:** Inconsistencies or generic domains (e.g., `...-lottery.org` instead of a recognized official lottery domain). 
* **Too Good to Be True:** Large unexpected windfalls are extremely rare and rarely communicated this way.
* **Urgency & Fear-based Language:** Phrases like "Hurry!" create pressure to act quickly without thinking. 
* **Suspicious Link:** Hovering over links reveals mismatched or non-official URLs. 
* **Generic Greeting:** Emails addressed generically or without your full name can be indicators.

**⚠️ Why It Matters:** These scams aim to trick you into sharing personal or financial details or paying a small 'fee' to 'claim' the non-existent prize. This information can then be used for identity theft.

**✅ Remediation & Prevention:**
* ❌ **NEVER** click links or open attachments in unsolicited win emails.
* 🛑 **Ignore unknown rewards:** If you didn't enter, you didn't win.
* 🔍 **Verify independently:** Use official websites and contact details, not any provided in the email.
* 📢 **Report the email** through official organizational channels.

---

### 2. 💼 Job Offer Scam Email (Phishing)

**Scenario:** An unsolicited email offers you a job, often with unrealistic compensation and little interview process, frequently involving paying initial fees.

**🚩 Key Phishing Indicators:**
* **Fake Sender Domain:** Mismatched or non-official company domains in the sender address. 
* **Too Good to Be True:** High salary for minimal effort, or a job that seems too good for your experience. 
* **Generic Greeting:** Addressed to "Dear Candidate" instead of your full name. 
* **Requests Money:** Legitimate employers *never* ask for payment for job placement, uniforms, or training. 
* **Urgency & Fear-based Language:** Pressure tactics to pay quickly.
* **Suspicious Link.**

**⚠️ Why It Matters:** Attackers steal your money via 'fees' or use your personal information (name, address, ID) for identity theft. 

**✅ Remediation & Prevention:**
* ❌ **NEVER** pay for a job.
* ❌ **NEVER** click suspicious links or attachments.
* 🔍 **Verify independently:** Go to the official company website, check their genuine careers page, or contact their HR department directly.
* 📢 **Report the email.**

---

### 3. 🔐 OTP Verification Scam Email (Phishing)

**Scenario:** You receive an email claiming to require your One-Time Password (OTP) or requesting verification to secure your account.

**🚩 Key Phishing Indicators:**
* **Fake Sender Domain:** Look for suspicious or mismatched domains in the sender's address (e.g., `...-secure-accoure-scourt.com`). 
* **Generic Greeting:** Addressed to "Dear User." 
* **Urgency & Fear-based Language:** Creating a sense of crisis ("OTP verification is required *immediately*"). 
* **Suspicious Link:** Mismatched or non-official URL. 

**⚠️ Why It Matters:** This type of phishing is extremely dangerous as it bypasses Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). If you provide the attacker with your OTP, they can log into your account immediately.

**✅ Remediation & Prevention:**
* ❌ **NEVER** share your OTP with *anyone* or enter it on *any* website linked from an email. 
* ❌ **Do NOT** click suspicious links.
* 🔍 **Verify Independently:** If you receive an unexpected OTP request, call IT security *directly* using verified internal numbers.
* 📢 **Report the email.**

---

### 4. 🏦 Bank Verification Email (Phishing)

**Scenario:** You receive an urgent email, supposedly from your bank, claiming unusual activity or security issues requiring immediate verification via a link.

**🚩 Key Phishing Indicators:**
* **Unknown Sender:** Misspellings in the sender name/address or a domain that doesn't match the actual bank. 
* **Generic Greeting:** Addressed to "Dear Customer." 
* **Urgency & Fear-based Language:** Creating a state of panic ("Failure to verify within 24 hours will result in permanent account lock"). 
* **Suspicious Link:** Mismatched or non-official banking URL. 

**⚠️ Why It Matters:** This is a classic, effective phishing technique. If you enter your banking details on the fake website, attackers gain full access to your funds.

**✅ Remediation & Prevention:**
* ❌ **NEVER** click banking links in emails. 
* 🔍 **Verify Independently:** *Always* access your online banking by typing the bank's official web address directly into your browser or using their official mobile app. 
* 📢 **Report the email.**

## 🚩 Common Phishing Indicators

Beyond the specific examples above, look out for:

| Indicator | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| 🚨 **Urgent or Threatening Tone** | Creating panic or a false sense of urgency to bypass critical thinking. |
| 👤 **Generic Greetings** | Emails addressed "Dear Customer" or "Dear User." |
| 🔗 **Suspicious Links** | Links that, when hovered over (without clicking!), reveal an unfamiliar or incorrect web address (Typosquatting). |
| 🕵️ **Fake Domains** | Email addresses with misspellings or domains that subtly mimic a real organization. |
| 📎 **Unexpected Attachments** | Attachments with common malware extensions (`.zip`, `.exe`) or unsolicited documents. |
| 💳 **Requests for Data** | Requests for passwords, OTPs, or full credit card details *via email*. |
| 📝 **Poor Grammar** | Many phishing emails contain notable spelling or grammatical errors. |

## 🎭 Other Types of Social Engineering & Phishing

Attackers use various forms of social engineering, including:

* 🎣 **Spear Phishing:** Highly targeted phishing directed at specific individuals or departments.
* 🐋 **Whaling:** Spear phishing targeting high-profile victims (CEOs, executives).
* 📱 **Smishing:** Phishing via SMS text messages.
* 📞 **Vishing:** Phishing via voice calls.
* 🎁 **Baiting:** Providing a false promise (e.g., a "free download") that delivers malware.

## 🛡️ Detailed Remediation & Prevention Guide

If you encounter a suspicious email:

**1. 🛑 STOP, LOOK, & THINK.**
   * Do *not* rush to act.
   * Look closely at the email details using the common indicators as your checklist.

**2. 🚫 DO NOT ENGAGE.**
   * **DO NOT** click links, buttons, or images. Hover over them to see destinations.
   * **DO NOT** open or download attachments.
   * **DO NOT** reply.

**3. 🔍 VERIFY INDEPENDENTLY.**
   * Go directly to the official website by typing the verified URL into your browser.
   * Contact the sender directly using official, verified telephone numbers.
